MenuCaptain

Privacy Policy

Effective June 29, 2026

MenuCaptain is a private notebook for eating out: you log restaurants and visits, scan menus, remember the dishes you loved, and (if you want) get AI-powered calorie estimates. This policy explains what information the service handles, why, and the choices you have. The short version: your food log is yours. We use your data to run the app for you — we do not sell it, and we do not show ads.

Information we collect

  • Account information. Your email address and a password. Passwords are stored only as secure cryptographic hashes by our authentication provider — we never see or store your plain password.
  • Your content. The restaurants, visits, dish ratings, notes, scanned menus, and photos you add to your log. This content is private to your account.
  • AI usage records. When you use an AI feature we record that a call happened, its size (token counts), and its estimated cost. We use this to enforce fair usage limits and protect the service from abuse.
  • Technical logs. Standard server logs (such as request timestamps and status codes) used to keep the service healthy and debug problems.

We do not collect advertising identifiers, we do not use analytics trackers, and we do not buy data about you from anyone.

How we use your information

  • To provide the service: storing your log, syncing it across your devices, and showing it back to you.
  • To power the AI features you invoke (described below).
  • To enforce usage limits, prevent abuse, and keep costs sustainable.
  • To respond when you contact us for support.

AI features

Some features send data to Anthropic (the AI provider) for processing, only when you use them: scanning a menu sends your menu photos; estimating a dish from a photo sends that photo; text-based estimates and “Help me order” send the relevant dish names, menu text, and your question. The results come back to your log. Under Anthropic’s API terms, this data is not used to train their models by default.

Service providers

We use a small set of providers to run MenuCaptain. Each receives only what it needs to do its job:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and photo storage (where your log and photos live).
  • Railway — hosts our backend server.
  • Anthropic — AI processing for the features above.
  • GitHub Pages — serves the app itself and any list pages you choose to publish.
  • Google — Google Places, for “Near me” discovery, looking a place up on Google, and showing a place’s rating and photos. The location, search terms, or place you request are sent to Google.
  • OpenStreetMap (Nominatim) — when you tap “Find address,” the restaurant name and city you typed are sent to look up its address and map location.
  • Serper — when you tap “See photos” on a dish, the dish name (and cuisine) is sent to find matching photos from the web.
  • Firecrawl — when you import a menu from a link, the page at that URL is fetched and rendered so the menu can be read.
  • Resend — sends account and notification emails (for example sign-in, password reset, and connection or share notifications) to your email address.
  • Stripe — payment processing for paid plans. Stripe handles your card details directly; we never see your full card number.

Sharing and published lists

Nothing in your log is shared unless you share it. If you use the share or publish features, the specific content you choose becomes visible to anyone who has the link or image — treat any shared link as public. What travels depends on what you share:

  • A place or list — the restaurant name, address, cuisine, and (if you include it) its menu.
  • A visit — the place, the date, the meal photos, the menu, and the names of the people you tagged as being there. It does not include your private notes, what you ate, your ratings, or any amount you spent.

Shared pages are hosted on our server (and shared list/menu pages on GitHub Pages) so the link works for whoever you send it to. Anyone the link is forwarded to can also open it, so only share what you’re comfortable being seen.

Data storage and security

Your data is stored in our hosted database and photo storage, isolated per account. Connections are encrypted in transit (HTTPS), access to your data requires your authenticated session, and provider credentials are kept on our servers — never in the app on your device. The app keeps a copy of your session and a local cache on your device so it loads fast; signing out clears them.

Retention and deletion

We keep your content for as long as your account is active so your log is there when you come back. To delete your account and its data, email us at support@menucaptain.com and we will remove it within 30 days. (In-app account deletion is coming; until then, email works.)

Children

MenuCaptain is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Health disclaimer

Calorie and macro figures produced by the AI features are estimates for curiosity and memory. They are not medical, dietary, or nutritional advice and should not be relied on for health decisions.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update this page and its effective date. Continued use of the service after a change means you accept the updated policy.
